6. Discussion


This paper developed techniques for analyzing the internal structure of distributed measurements. We introduced entanglement, which quantifies the extent to which a measurement is indecomposable. Entanglement can be shown to quantify context-dependence. Moreover, positive entanglement is necessary for a system to generate more information than the sum of its subsystems. Along the way, we constructed the quale, which geometrically represents the compositional structure of a distributed measurement. The information-theoretic approach developed here is dual, in a precise sense, to the algorithmic perspective on computation.
